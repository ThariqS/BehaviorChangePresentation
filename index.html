Unlocking Sleep

Thariq Shihipar, Sara Taylor

Motivation

  1. Sleep is important, and everyone is diffent (n=1) but planning helps
  2. Planning requires logging
  3. Logging is hard

We think we can use our unlocks screens to log data & give us feedback on sleep planning.

System Overview

How can we scale n-of-1 experiments?

  • Real-time data collection & analysis
  • Dynamic feedback on questions
  • Build to generalizable for any behavior change

Technology

  1. Firebase
  2. Android App
  3. Web-interfae

Study Design & Deployment

We recruited 19 MIT students with Android phones.

6 males, 13 females.

Study Outline

Week 1 Fill out daily Google Surveys through email
Weeks 2-3 Use the app, answer static questions while unlocking
Week 4 Questions become dynamic based on data collected

Questions asked:

(at most 10 questions were asked per day)
  1. When do you plan to wake up & go to sleep
  2. When did you actually wake up & go to sleep
  3. How are you feeling about:
    1. Stress
    2. Health
    3. Alertness
    4. Mood

Acutal Data Collected

  1. When & where users unlocked their phones
  2. Answers to every question (from this we extrapolate health information)
  3. How long it took to answer questions

Data Collected

  • Days of Data: 442
  • Times Unlocked: 19176
  • Questions Asked: 7511
  • Avg Unlocks/day: 43

Results

People aren't good at planning for sleep

“For years there has been a theory that millions of monkeys typing at random on millions of typewriters would reproduce the entire works of Shakespeare. The Internet has proven this theory to be untrue.”

We can help people think about sleep

“I like the app in general because I try to track my sleeping patterns, and this helps me remember my sleep by asking oftenâ€¦”

Unlocking is a very automatic action

“I found myself swiping to unlock without noticing that there was a question”

Things we don't know

  • How can we give feedback using more than just questions?
  • Is it a design problem or an application problem?

Conclusions

  • Built a scalable system for asking health related questions
  • Ran a pilot study
  • Got some encouraging data, but mostly inconclusive due to implementation & scale

We're gonna do this again

  1. Improved design & reliability
  2. More feedback systems
  3. Purely digital consent & installation
